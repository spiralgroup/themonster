There are many kinds of discounts and offers you can use on your Spiral Group, Inc. Special Offers WebPage. Below is a list of the ones we believe will help guide you in the most profitable direction.

⚬ Percentage-Based Discounts

This can include small incentive percentages like 5% or 10% off, larger discounts to really drive sales like 20% and 25%, or significant percentages like 50%+ to liquidate merchandise that’s old or isn’t moving.


⚬ Free Shipping Offers

Shipping costs are one major reason for shopping cart abandonment. Offering free shipping is a great way to mitigate this and increase conversions. You can use free shipping offers in conjunction with a minimum purchase requirement to increase your average order value.


⚬ Free Gift Offers

A free gift with a purchase can be a great way to provide additional value to the customer. If used strategically, it can also be used to increase average order size and/or to get rid of product that isn’t moving.


⚬ Weekly & Monthly Discounts

Traditional sales used to drive conversions. The online photo department at Walgreens has weekly sales.


⚬ Prelaunch Offers

If you’re in the prelaunch stage of your Small Business or launching a new product or service, you can use prelaunch offers to drive traffic and peak interest, turning new customers into loyal customers.

Consider offering free gift cards in exchange for customers’ email addresses and time to fill out a brief survey. You can kick off this initiative by manually reaching out to potential respondents on your social networks.


⚬ Holiday & Seasonal Deals

Throughout the entire year there are national and commemorative holidays that provide an opportunity to share relevant discounts and offers with customers. Online retailer Blu Skin Care regularly makes use of holidays to promote offers to their visitors and past customers. They promote these using email and social media.


⚬ Email/Newsletter Subscription Offer

Building an email list is important for successful online retailers. By providing an offer in exchange for a visitor's email address, you increase the chance of a conversion. Consider offering 10% off their first order for new subscribers to your email list. Used strategically, your list can be invaluable for increasing brand awareness, fostering customer loyalty, and meeting sales goals.


⚬ Social Media Incentives

One of the most challenging aspects of operating a new online store is getting the word out. Giving visitors and customers an incentive to share your store within their social circles can be an effective way to create a buzz around your brand. Online retailer Truxx offers customers $5 off their next purchase in exchange for visitors sharing about the brand and experience on Facebook.


⚬ Referral Promos

People are more likely to purchase from you if referred by a friend or family member. Use this to your advantage by encouraging referrals. You can choose to give a deal to the person referring, the person being referred, or both.


⚬ First-Time Shopper Offer

This kind of promotion could be the nudge a first-time visitor needs to be converted into a paying customer. Online retailer Clearly provides all new visitors with a 50% discount on prescription glasses, 15% on contact lenses, and free shipping. DODOcase, an online manufacturer of iPad cases, offers 10% off your first purchase.


⚬ Minimum Purchase Discount

An offer based on the total value of a shopping cart is an effective upselling tactic to encourage customers to spend more. You can offer minimum purchase discounts for your products and services.


⚬ Exclusive Social Offers

Exclusive offers on your social networks can be a great way to build customer loyalty with those who follow you. This tactic also provides a reason for new people to follow and subscribe to your social channels, allowing you to market to them in the future.


⚬ Customer Loyalty Offers

Rewarding customer loyalty is crucial. This can be accomplished by simply sending your best customers a personal email containing a discount or credit.


⚬ Coupons for Purchasing In-Store

Just as you can drive online sales with offers, you can also drive in-store sales. This can be at your brick-and-mortar location, or at in-person events such as festivals, fairs, expos, trade shows, etc. Use your email list as a marketing tool to send coupons to subscribers containing discounts for in-store purchases. Consider accepting these coupons even after the expiration date.


⚬ Event Attendance Offers

If you host events, you can reward attendees with discounts or free gifts. This can help cultivate the relationship with engaged customers, increase customer loyalty, and encourage attendance for your next event.


⚬ Customer Milestone Discounts

These milestones could be personal to a customer such as a birthday or wedding anniversary. Other milestones can celebrate the relationship you’ve built with one another — the anniversary of the customer’s first purchase or spending a certain amount of money. Consider rewarding your customers when they have reached certain milestones with extra discounts and free gifts.
