COURSE OVERVIEW
In this class, food-service workers learn how to properly prepare and serve food. The information imparted in this program is based on the FDA food code, and is meant to provide basic guidelines for safe food handling. Although this course has no official prerequisites and may be completed by any person seeking knowledge of safe food handling procedures, it is required for food service workers employed in restaurants, bars, nursing homes, licensed day care facilities, hospitals, schools, and long-term care facilities. HOW DO I GET MY RESULTS/CERTIFICATE?
If you took an Online Course and Assessment, your results will be available immediately after passing your assessment. You can print and view your certificate at the ServSafe® website. If you took an Instructor led class and provided a valid email address on your answer sheet, you’ll receive an email notice when your results are available.

HOW DOES THE SERVSAFE® FOOD HANDLER CERTIFICATE DIFFER FROM THE SERVSAFE® FOOD PROTECTION MANAGER CERTIFICATION?
The ServSafe® Food Handler Certificate verifies basic food safety knowledge and is for individuals in food handler employee-level positions. Upon successful completion of the ANSI ASTM 2659 accredited Food Handler course and 40-question assessment, the employee will receive a Certificate of Achievement from the National Restaurant Association that can be printed from a secure section of the ServSafe® website.

The ServSafe® Food Protection Manager Certification verifies that a manager, or person-in-charge, has sufficient food safety knowledge to protect the public from foodborne illness. Individuals that successfully pass the exam will receive a ServSafe® Food Protection Manager Certification. The ServSafe® Food Protection Manager Certification is accredited by the American National Standards Institute (ANSI) under the Conference for Food Protection Standards.
