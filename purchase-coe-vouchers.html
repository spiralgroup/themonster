DEBUNKING THE MYTH

The Illinois state prison system requires inmates to acquire and maintain employment during their entire period of incarceration. It is not unusual for inmates to contribute to the overall profitability of the prison system, as well as assist with achieving and maintaining Operational Excellence for the Warden. Therefore, there aren't any employment gaps from the time the person is incarcerated until he is released from the prison system. Employment gaps usually occur after an inmate has been released from the prison system because not many employers will hire them due to a crminal past.

In fact, many incarcerated individuals may have learned new skills that are easily transferable to your organization. It is unlikely that people with criminal records, who are also qualified to execute the duties of the positions you're seeking to fill, will return to a life of crime if allowed to earn a living with an Employer of Choice.
