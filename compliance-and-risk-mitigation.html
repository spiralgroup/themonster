EMPLOYMENT DISCRIMINATION

OVERVIEW

Discriminatory Employment Practices include biased behavior in recruiting, hiring, job assignment, compensation, promotion, testing, training, apprenticeships, internships, and termination processes. Employment discrimination occurs silently in the beliefs, attitudes, and values that one applies in his mind toward a job candidate. Although discriminatory words may never be uttered, the person prone to discriminate will use non-discriminatory tactics to 'legally' reject a qualified job seeker.

After Completing This Course, You're Expected To

Define discrimination and disability, as outlined by the law and the ADA.
Identify when a job opportunity has been denied to a person based on his relationship to, or association with, people included in the protected classifications.
Describe the types of discrimination disabled people are protected against.


Who Should Enroll?

Employers, Employment Recruiters, Hiring Decision-Makers, Workforce Managers, Social Workers, Case Managers, Persons Responsible for Conducting Interviews on the Behalf on an Employer, Staffing Agency Personnel

WORKPLACE HARASSMENT

OVERVIEW

The EEOC defines harassment as unwelcome verbal, physical, and sexual behavior. Harassing conduct includes offensive jokes, slurs, name calling, physical assaults or threats, intimidation, ridicule, insults, offensive pictures, and more. The harasser can be a supervisor, co-worker, customer, vendor, etc. The victim doesn't even have to be the person being harassed. It can be anyone adversely affected by harassing behavior. In this course, students learn how to recognize and address harassment.

After Completing This Course, You're Expected To

Define harassment in all its forms.
Understand the devastating effects harassment can have on victims, witnesses, and businesses.
Recognize and address harassment.
Know common prevention strategies against harassment.
Analyze his own behavior to ensure it is appropriate at all times.

DIVERSITY & INCLUSION

OVERVIEW

If your assumptions about certain groups of people are either based on what you've heard from others, or what you've learned through the media, then it's possible those assumptions are incorrect. Sometimes, our beliefs are merely the result of cultural conditioning. In this course, workplace professionals are encouraged to begin accepting that we can all work to become more aware of how our inner thoughts, feelings, and assumptions affect our beliefs, actions, and even our inactions.

After Completing This Course, You're Expected To

Define Worplace Diversity.
Understand the devastating effects stereotypes can have on us all.
Spend time observing the attitudes and behaviors that contribute to the exclusion of others.
Work to prevent behaviors that can be perceived as offensive.
