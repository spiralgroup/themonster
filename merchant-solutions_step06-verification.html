Your Special Offers

If you start with a clear goal in mind, firm understanding of your brand, and willingness to experiment, you can effectively use the Special Offers section to meet your objectives, build customer loyalty, and increase revenues.

• BOPIS

If your goal is to compete directly against another competitive alternative, then consider applying a competitive strategy by creating a latent sales opportunity. For example, offer BOPIS (Buy Online, Pickup In-Store) opportunities to increase sales. Train your staff to leverage these interactions with customers by creating a pleasant and personalized in-store experience for the online shopper. Begin by pre-planning what could/should be suggested with the item(s) being retrieved from your retail location.


• Minimum Purchase Discount

If your goal is to position yourself as a higher-end brand, then consider opportunities where you can provide a high-value solution. Using minimum purchase discounts is an effective upselling tactic to encourage customers to spend more. You can offer minimum purchase discounts for specific products and services, or free shipping on all orders over $100.


• Percentage-Based Discounts

This can include small incentive percentages like 5% or 10% off, larger discounts to really drive sales like 20% and 25%, or significant percentages like 50%+ to liquidate merchandise that’s old or isn’t moving.


• Free Gift Offers

A free gift with a purchase can be a great way to provide additional value to the customer. If used strategically, it can also be used to increase average order size and/or to get rid of product that isn’t moving.


• Weekly & Monthly Discounts

Traditional sales used to drive conversions. The online photo department at Walgreens has weekly sales.


• Holiday & Seasonal Deals

Throughout the entire year there are national and commemorative holidays that provide opportunities to share relevant discounts and offers with customers.


• Email/Newsletter Subscription Offer

Building an email list is important for successful online retailers. By providing an offer in exchange for a visitor's email address, you increase the chance of a conversion. Used strategically, your list can be invaluable for increasing brand awareness, fostering customer loyalty, and meeting sales goals.


• Referral Promos

People are more likely to purchase from you if referred by a friend or family member. Use this to your advantage by encouraging referrals. You can choose to give a deal to the person referring, the person being referred, or both.


• Exclusive Social Offers

Exclusive offers on your social networks can be a great way to build customer loyalty with those who follow you. This tactic also provides a reason for new people to follow and subscribe to your social channels, allowing you to market to them in the future.


• Customer Loyalty Offers

Rewarding customer loyalty is crucial. This can be accomplished by simply sending your best customers a personal email containing a discount or credit.


• Coupons for Purchasing In-Store

Just as you can drive online sales with offers, you can also drive in-store sales. This can be at your brick-and-mortar location, or at in-person events such as festivals, fairs, expos, trade shows, etc. Use your email list as a marketing tool to send coupons to subscribers containing discounts for in-store purchases. Consider accepting these coupons even after the expiration date.


• Event Attendance Offers

If you host events, you can reward attendees with discounts or free gifts. This can help cultivate the relationship with engaged customers, increase customer loyalty, and encourage attendance for your next event.


• Customer Milestone Discounts

These milestones could be personal to a customer such as a birthday or wedding anniversary. Other milestones can celebrate the relationship you’ve built with one another — the anniversary of the customer’s first purchase or spending a certain amount of money. Consider rewarding your customers when they have reached certain milestones with extra discounts and free gifts.


• You're Ready

Ultimately, you'll need to consider the kind of offers that are right for your brand. This will involve a lot of experimentation to understand what works best. Create a goal for every offer, start small, and measure the results.
