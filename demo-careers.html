6 Tips for a Great Careers WebPage

As a small business owner, you can quickly reach a local audience through your Small Business Showcase WebPages. Promote and sell your products and services while using your Careers WebPage to recruit and sell your brand to local job seekers.

This can be the place where you differentiate your organization from competitors and really sell the company as a place where potential employees may want to work. It's a section within your company's digital brochure and can work to compliment your efforts in social media and other recruitment strategies.

In the above section we have provided our own current job posting, but it's merely an example of how we have chosen to use this web page in our demo. When you purchase our Essentials or Premium WebPages Plan, you decide how you'll use this web page to your company's advantage. The following suggestions can help shape your Careers WebPage so people will want to work with you.

1. Develop a plan of how you'll want to use this web page. Will you be posting jobs only? Do you want to add a company career blog, produce video testimonials with current employers, discussing why they work with your company? What type of content do you want — employee testimonials, awards, company news, a blog?

If you use social media, we already include the social media icons (see footer section) on each of our Small Business Showcase WebPages so people can connect with you and follow you. You can also use your social media platforms to direct people to the Small Business Showcase WebPages you want to highlight.

Social media platforms such as Facebook, Twitter, and LinkedIn are inexpensive and easy ways to connect with future candidates. Use them to share company success stories, promote the company brand, and sell the business as a place to work. Use these resources to your full advantage. If this is a task that is too big for current staff, consider hiring a social media intern to help.

2. What other information would you like to include? Perhaps information on campus recruiting visits or when your company will be at job fairs. What about a message (video) from the company president?

3. Include a “Reasons to Join Our Team” Section. (A) Work from home two days a week. (B) We offer free childcare. (C) We have a team night out every month. (D) We have an excellent training program. (E) We’ve just opened three new locations. (F) We're an industry leader. The best candidates are often concerned about benefits, career advancement, training, and industry reputation.

4. Show diversity by making your web page feel inviting to people of all backgrounds — like almost anyone would fit neatly within your company culture. Make it interesting for current job seekers and intriguing for future job seekers.

5. Provide information on how to apply for jobs and how you prefer to hear from candidates. Those who visit your Careers WebPage may not be looking for a job at all, but they may learn something new about the company and pass on information to someone who is actively seeking employment.

6. Look at what your competitors are doing. Do you see something you like and could incorporate onto your Careers WebPage? What can be added to yours that keeps you on track with competitors, or give you the edge?

Discover the right blend of content, visuals, and video, and we can help your Careers WebPage appeal to a larger local audience. Keep in mind that you’re selling to job seekers on why they should want to work for you as much as they're supposed to sell themselves to you on why you should hire them. Following these tips can help differentiate your small business from competitors and sell the company as the place where top talent wants to work.
